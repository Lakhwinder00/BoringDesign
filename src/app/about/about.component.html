BoringOMeter

The BoringOMeter is a web app that allows you to measure and rate the visual complexity of a building's design.

It enables users to assess the complexity of a building's facade and score it on a scale of one to ten. A complex facade, such as the Sagrada Familia in Barcelona, scores 10 out of 10 (good, according to the BoringOMeter), while the Seagram Building in New York scores 2 out of 10 (bad, according to the BoringOMeter).

This BoringOMeter app is produced by Heatherwick Studio and MKS DTECH as part of the Humanise campaign, a decade-long initiative that seeks to inspire a movement to stop the creation of boring buildings.

We believe that we are all living through a quiet, unnoticed global catastrophe, in which many towns and cities have become miserable and soulless due to the proliferation of featureless, uninteresting buildings.

Research shows that these places make people unhappy, stressed, and sick. Boring buildings are also unloved and often demolished, only to be replaced with equally unremarkable buildings, which worsens the climate crisis.

We hope to change this. Buildings have the power to inspire and uplift us, making people feel safe, playful, romantic, and awestruck, just as they can also make us feel numb, sad, and disconnected from our surroundings.

You can use the BoringOMeter for free and learn more about the Humanise campaign at ihumanize.org.
